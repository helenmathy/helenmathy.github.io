Neptune

Interesting facts

Rank - 8th and final planet in our solar system. The solar system's outermost gas giant remains invisible to the naked eye. A telescope is required to simply catch a view of it. Due to its vast distance from the sun, Neptune is mainly left dark, incredibly cold and windy. Winds of over 2,000 km/h ravage the planet. Comparatively, the most powerful winds on Earth reach up to 400 km/h. Atmospherically it contains hydrogen, helium and methane. It has a solid core which is the same size as Earth. It’s mass consists of frozen water, ammonia and methane. Its blue colour is courtesy of the high levels of methane in its atmosphere. Surrounding Neptune are six rings, but unlike Saturn’s rings these are very difficult to see.

A Neptunian day lasts the equivalent of 16 Earth hours and a year lasts 165 Earth years.
Named after the Greek god of water, 13 moons are in orbit around Neptune, each one named aptly after mythological Greek Gods, Goddesses and nymphs.

  • Claim to Fame - The Windiest Planet.
  • Planet Type - Ice Giant.
  • Length of Year - 60,190 Earth Days, or 165 Earth years.
