Mercury

Interesting facts

Rank - 1st in our Solar System and therefore closest planet to the Sun, a mere 36 million miles or 56 million kilometers . It is the smallest planet in our solar system with a circumference marginally larger than Earth's Moon. The sun would appear 3 times larger on Mercury than it does on Earth.

At first glance, the passing of time can seem rather distorted on Mercury. Daylight hours for a single day last the equivalent of 59 Earth days. The time it takes Mercury to spin once on its axis (aka day-night cycle) is equivalent to 175.9 Earth day-night cycles. Whereas a full Mercurian year is equivalent to 88 Earth days. Hence it takes only marginally less time for one full day-night cycle than it does for Mercury to travel twice around the sun. Mercury has neither rings nor moon. It is a solid planet with a solid metal core. To date there is no evidence that life ever existed on Mercury. Daytime Temperatures of 430 degrees Celsius (800 degrees Fahrenheit) make it inhospitable as does the nighttime temperature which drops to a mere -180 degrees Celsius (-290 degrees Fahrenheit). The atmosphere on Mercury is very thin and comprises oxygen, hydrogen, helium, sodium and potassium. Due to its thin atmosphere, these atom particles are blown into the surrounding space by solar winds. Mercury’s exosphere is cratered from micro-meteoroid impacts and resembles that of Earth’s moon.
